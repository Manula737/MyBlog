The 2026 Toyota Camry is a fully redesigned, hybrid-only sedan that has already launched in some markets and features a sportier design, advanced safety features, and improved fuel efficiency. In the US, it is expected to launch nationwide in spring 2026. Key Features and Specifications The 2026 Toyota Camry is part of the ninth generation and comes exclusively with a hybrid powertrain in the US and Canada. Powertrain: It utilizes the fifth-generation Toyota Hybrid System (THS 5) pairing a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine with electric motors. Performance: The front-wheel drive (FWD) configuration produces a combined 225 horsepower, while the available Electronic On-Demand All-Wheel Drive (AWD) version delivers 232 horsepower. Fuel Efficiency: The FWD models achieve an impressive estimated 4.9 L/100 km (city/highway combined). Exterior Design: The vehicle features a sleek, aerodynamic design with a "hammerhead" front fascia, sharp LED headlights, and a fastback roofline. A new Nightshade Edition is available, adding blacked-out exterior accents and 19-inch satin-black wheels. Interior Features: The cabin has been upgraded with quality materials and modern technology, including standard dual-zone automatic climate control, heated front seats, and an 8-inch touchscreen multimedia system (a 12.3-inch screen is available on higher trims). Technology and Safety The 2026 Camry is equipped with a range of advanced technology and safety features. Infotainment: Features include wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto compatibility, multiple USB-C ports, and an available premium 9-speaker JBL audio system. Toyota Safety Sense 3.0 (TSS 3.0): This suite of standard safety features includes a Pre-Collision System with pedestrian and daytime bicycle detection, Full-Speed Dynamic Radar Cruise Control, Lane Departure Alert with Steering Assist, and Lane Tracing Assist. Driver-Assist Features: Higher trims offer available features like Traffic Jam Assist (requires a subscription to Drive Connect) and a Panoramic View Monitor.

The 2026 Toyota Corolla features minor updates for the current generation, including standard new technology and safety features across all trims, and is available in both gasoline and hybrid powertrains in various markets. Sales commenced in March 2026 in some local markets, with arrival at US dealerships expected in the fall of 2026. Key Features and Specifications The 2026 Toyota Corolla continues to be a core model, with a focus on technology, safety, and efficiency. Powertrain Options: Gasoline: A 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine is available, producing 169 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. Hybrid: A 1.8-liter hybrid system delivers 138 system horsepower and offers excellent fuel efficiency, with all-wheel drive (AWD) available on some hybrid trims in certain markets (e.g., Hybrid LE in Canada). Technology: Digital Displays: A 7-inch digital gauge cluster is now standard on gas models, while a larger 12.3-inch version is standard on the XSE grade and optional for the hybrid SE. Infotainment: All models come standard with the Toyota Multimedia system via an 8-inch touchscreen, with available upgrades to a 10.5-inch or 12.9-inch display on higher trims, featuring wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto. Safety: All trims come standard with Toyota Safety Sense 3.0 (TSS 3.0). Blind Spot Monitoring with Rear Cross-Traffic Alert is a newly standard feature across all grades. Exterior: Exterior styling updates include a bolder front end with slimmer LED headlights in some markets. New paint options have been added, including an exclusive matte Onyx Grey finish for GR Sport models in Europe.

Lamborghini's current lineup for 2026 includes three main production models: the V12 hybrid Revuelto supercar, the V8 hybrid Temerario supercar (successor to the Huracán), and the Urus SE plug-in hybrid SUV. The company is also producing exclusive limited-run "few-off" models like the Fenomeno. Current Lamborghini Production Models (2026) Lamborghini is moving into a fully electrified era, with all current models featuring some form of hybrid technology. Lamborghini Revuelto: This is Lamborghini's flagship V12 hybrid supercar (HPEV - High Performance Electrified Vehicle) and the successor to the Aventador. Powertrain: It combines a naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V12 engine with three electric motors for a total output of 1,015 horsepower (1015 CV). Performance: It can accelerate from 0 to 62 mph (100 km/h) in 2.5 seconds and has a top speed of over 217 mph (>350 km/h). Lamborghini Temerario: The Temerario is the replacement for the popular V10 Huracán and is Lamborghini's second plug-in hybrid supercar (HPEV). Powertrain: It is equipped with a new 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 engine paired with three electric motors, generating a combined total power of 920 horsepower (920 CV). The V8 engine itself revs up to an impressive 10,000 rpm. Performance: It has a 0-62 mph (100 km/h) acceleration time of 2.7 seconds and a top speed of 213 mph (343 km/h). Lamborghini Urus SE: The Urus SE is the first Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV) version of the Urus Super SUV and the most powerful Urus ever. Powertrain: The re-engineered 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 engine and electric motor system produce a combined 800 horsepower (800 CV). It offers an all-electric driving range of over 60 km. Performance: It accelerates from 0 to 62 mph (100 km/h) in 3.4 seconds and has a top speed of 194 mph (312 km/h). Limited Production Models Lamborghini occasionally produces exclusive, limited-run vehicles for collectors. Lamborghini Fenomeno: This "few-off" model is based on the Revuelto's V12 hybrid powertrain and is the most powerful Lamborghini yet, with 1,065 horsepower. Only 29 units are planned for production.
