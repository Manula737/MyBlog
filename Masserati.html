Maserati is a renowned Italian luxury vehicle manufacturer known for its stylish design, high performance, and exclusivity. Founded in 1914, Maserati has a rich heritage in motorsports and has produced a range of iconic cars over the years. The brand is recognized for its elegant aesthetics, powerful engines, and advanced technology, catering to enthusiasts who seek a blend of luxury and sportiness. The company, part of the Stellantis Group, is in the process of electrifying its entire lineup with the "Folgore" series of electric vehicles. Current Maserati Lineup Maserati's current 2025-2026 model lineup blends traditional internal combustion engines (ICE) with new mild-hybrid and full-electric (Folgore) powertrains. Maserati Grecale: This compact luxury SUV is an entry point to the brand's SUV offerings. It is available with a 2.0-liter mild-hybrid engine (300-330 hp) and a high-performance Trofeo trim featuring a 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 engine (523 hp). The fully electric Grecale Folgore model, with a combined output of around 542 horsepower and an estimated range of 310 miles, is also available. Maserati Levante: The brand's larger three-row luxury SUV. It offers V6 and V8 engine options, with the top-tier Trofeo Ultima trim producing 580 horsepower from its twin-turbo V8. Production of the Levante ended in 2024, but models may still be available at dealerships. Maserati Ghibli: A luxury sport sedan known for its balanced performance and Italian styling. For 2025, it is offered primarily with a twin-turbo V6 engine. Maserati Quattroporte: The flagship luxury sedan offering refinement and performance. Maserati GranTurismo: This iconic grand tourer coupe made a return for 2025 and features four seats and all-wheel drive. It comes in Modena and Trofeo trims with a V6 "Nettuno" engine (490 hp and 550 hp, respectively). The all-electric GranTurismo Folgore features an 800-volt architecture and three motors, delivering up to 751 hp and capable of a 0-60 mph time of 2.7 seconds. Maserati GranCabrio: The convertible version of the GranTurismo, also available in a V6-powered Trofeo and a fully electric GranCabrio Folgore variant. Maserati MC20: A mid-engined supercar that serves as Maserati's halo performance vehicle.
